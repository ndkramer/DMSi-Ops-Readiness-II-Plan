This document maps the 7 stages (WM-S0-WM-S6) of the constraint model to the 9 outcomes (WM-OC-01 through WM-OC-09) of the outcome model. The goal is to determine whether both models can coexist -- the team delivers on outcomes while the PM reports on constraint stages -- or whether the mapping is too complex to maintain.

Key Findings

Strong Alignment (4 of 7 stages = 57%)
WM-S2, WM-S4, WM-S5, and WM-S6 all map cleanly to individual outcomes. The scope, deliverables, and exit criteria are nearly identical. Reporting on one translates directly to the other. WM-S2 required a prerequisite correction (copy/paste bug from WM-S4), but the scope is the same.
WM-S3 is the Only Split
WM-S3 (System Configured and Ready) maps to 2 outcomes: WM-OC-03 (tool setup) and WM-OC-04 (queue disposition). The constraint model bundles these together, but queue disposition of 559 open 360 cases is a distinct deliverable that can run in parallel with early adoption. Status rollup requires aggregation.
Two New Outcomes Fill Real Gaps
WM-OC-06 (Full Category Coverage) fills the gap between "team using the system" and "board is accurate" with Phase 4.1-4.3 work. WM-OC-08 (Scorecard) captures flow metrics and Workstream D integration. Both are additive -- they don't change the constraint model, they surface work it doesn't represent.

Reporting Translation: How it Works in Practice

Bottom line: The mapping is clean enough that both models can coexist. The PM reports on constraint stages using status data from outcome deliverables. One complication exists (WM-S3 split), and it is manageable. The WM-S0+WM-S1 merge is cosmetic -- both stages come from the same outcome.

Deep Dive: The WM-S3 Split

WM-S3 is the only stage that requires translation effort. Here is exactly how the 2 outcomes roll up:

WM-S3 Scope Area Outcome Can complete independently? WM-S3 reporting impact
Tool evaluation, selection, and board configuration WM-OC-03 YES When WM-OC-03 is done, report WM-S3 tool setup as complete
559 open 360 cases: review, categorize, migrate or close WM-OC-04 YES (after WM-OC-03 exists) When WM-OC-04 is done, report WM-S3 queue disposition as complete. WM-S3 stage = done.
The WM-S3 reporting advantage: In the constraint model, WM-S3 looks like one block. In reality, queue disposition (WM-OC-04) can begin as soon as the tool exists and run in parallel with initial adoption (WM-OC-05). The outcome model shows this overlap; the constraint model hides it. When reporting WM-S3 to leadership, you can show sub-deliverable progress that the outcome model makes visible.

Deep Dive: The WM-S0+WM-S1 Merge

WM-S0 and WM-S1 are the simplest mapping. Both serve one purpose -- "understand how work flows today" -- and both are fully covered by WM-OC-01.

Constraint Stage What It Covered Where It Lives in WM-OC-01
WM-S0: Access and Engagement Engineering team access, stakeholder introductions, discovery session scheduling WM-OC-01.1 (Discovery Sessions with Engineering Team)
WM-S1: Current State Documented Intake channel mapping, tracking mechanism inventory, pain point documentation, volume/mix analysis, 360 case evaluation WM-OC-01.2 (Workflow Mapping), WM-OC-01.3 (360 Case Evaluation), WM-OC-01.4 (Current State Assessment)
Reporting impact: When leadership asks "Is WM-S0 done?" or "Is WM-S1 done?", the answer comes from WM-OC-01 status. Both are complete when WM-OC-01 is complete. No translation needed.
