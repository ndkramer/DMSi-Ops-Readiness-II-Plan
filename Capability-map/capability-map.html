DMSi Version

⚙ Work Management

On Track ▾

Engineering leaders and team members have a single view of all planned work, current capacity, and can make prioritization decisions with confidence they'll be executed. Work is visible, sequenced, and tracked.

What customers notice: Requests don't disappear. Commitments made are commitments kept. When priorities shift, customers hear about it proactively.

Future State

◉ Visibility Infrastructure

On Track ▾

Engineering, leadership, and support teams see real-time system health through dashboards they trust and reference daily. Anomalies are detected and alerts fire before customers raise issues. The team responds to signals from the system, not complaints from customers.

What customers notice: DMSi reaches out about issues before customers report them. When customers do call, support already knows what's happening and can provide status immediately. Issues get diagnosed faster because the team isn't starting from scratch each time.

Future State

⚙ Pipeline Automation

Discovery ▾

Configuration and code changes flow through automated pipelines that are tested, approved, and reversible without manual intervention. This level of rigor is applied consistently to all internal environments and production systems. The process is repeatable, auditable, and standard for both platform and development teams.

What customers notice: Fewer "something changed and now it's broken" incidents. When changes do cause issues, resolution is faster because the team knows exactly what changed and can roll it back quickly. Maintenance windows become shorter and less disruptive.

Future State

📚 Learning System

Discovery ▾

The organization learns from incidents systematically. An observability platform provides integrated SLOs, error budgets, and alerting. Incidents are categorized consistently and responses follow documented playbooks. Root cause analysis drives fixes that eliminate recurring problems. The environment improves through deliberate process, not heroic effort.

What customers notice: Problems that used to recur stop happening. The platform feels more stable over time, not just "patched until next time." When incidents do occur, resolution is consistent regardless of who's on call. Customers develop confidence that DMSi is actively improving, not just maintaining.

Future State

👥 B: Customer Support Operating Model

Discovery ▾

Product pods own 80% of cases with deep module expertise. Support/Engineering/Product triads coordinate with clear accountability -- cases never fall into a black hole after handoff. Incidents are managed with consistent discipline. MBM owns customer communications. RCAs are completed within 5 business days, and learnings translate into prevention. Engineering escalations reduced 50%. MTTR measured in hours, not days.

What customers notice: Strategic accounts no longer wonder if their issues are being taken seriously. When they call, they're routed to someone who knows their environment and history. When an incident occurs, they hear from DMSi first -- not the other way around. Resolution times are measured in hours, not days. For commercial customers, the experience becomes consistent and predictable.

Future State

🛠 C: Support & Engineering Tooling Strategy

Discovery ▾

Tooling supports the full case lifecycle, product pods, and incident tracking in one integrated platform. Cases route automatically by product area and customer segment. The tooling triggers KCS notification and reduces inter-team friction. Automated contributions remove manual handling effort. 10-15% efficiency beyond operating model KPIs.

What customers notice: Support interactions feel faster and more informed. Less "let me check and get back to you" and more immediate context. When they call about an issue, support already has visibility -- no tickets disappearing in the service desk. Communications are answered proactively and consistently -- not dependent on which agent happens to pick up the phone. Over time, issues that used to require a call now resolve through self-service or are fixed before they notice.

Future State

📊 D: Strategic Visibility

On Track ▾

Instead of discovering risk through outages, escalations, and churn, leadership sees it forming and can intervene early. Leaders have one view of reality, trusted by everyone who needs to make decisions, and they can see what's actually happening -- not individual narratives. Data is actionable, strategically aligned, and has the context necessary to make decisions. Opinions are tested against data. DMSi builds the muscle of looking at the numbers first, then deciding.

Future State
