Key Findings

Clean Mappings (60%)

S3, S4, S5, S7, S8, and S9 all map 1:1 to outcomes. Same scope, same deliverables, same risks. Status reporting for these stages can be read directly from the corresponding outcome with zero translation. This is the majority of the work.

Structural Splits (S0, S2, S6)

Three stages split into multiple outcomes. S2 is the most complex (3 outcomes). These splits are justified: they unlock parallel execution (log migration, synthetic monitoring) and separate concerns (baselines vs. decommission). The PM must aggregate outcome status when reporting stage progress.

New Outcomes (VI-OC-13, VI-OC-15, VI-OC-16)

Three outcomes have no stage equivalent. VI-OC-13 (A2W Readiness) consolidates scattered A2W tasks. VI-OC-15 (Legacy Fallback) makes the "what if validation fails" path explicit. VI-OC-16 (PagerDuty AIOps) captures a full deployment lifecycle that the constraint model only referenced as a single work package (WP 6.7). All are additive -- they don't change the constraint model.

Deep Dive: Non-1:1 Mappings

SPLIT S0: Foundation and Plan Alignment -> VI-OC-01 + VI-OC-02

To VI-OC-01 (Foundation Planning): WP 0.1 (Inventory), WP 0.2 (Architecture Design), WP 0.4 (Integration Architecture), WP 0.5 (A2W Telemetry Strategy), WP 0.6 (Script Rationalization), WP 0.7 (Elastic Inventory)
To VI-OC-02 (Telemetry & Licensing): WP 0.3 (Licensing and Procurement) -- merged with all S1 work packages
Why split: Licensing (WP 0.3) shares a dependency chain with S1 telemetry work -- both must resolve before deployment. Keeping them together in VI-OC-02 reflects the real sequence. The other S0 work packages are purely planning with no procurement dependency.
Parallel opportunity: VI-OC-01 planning work and VI-OC-02 telemetry evaluation can overlap. In the stage-gate model, S1 waits for all of S0.

MERGED S1: Telemetry Strategy Validated -> VI-OC-02

All S1 work packages absorbed into VI-OC-02: WP 1.1 (ABL Telemetry Eval), WP 1.2 (OTel Feasibility), WP 1.3 (Log Retention), WP 1.4 (Licensing Procurement), WP 1.5 (RBAC), WP 1.6 (New Relic Scope)
Why merge: S1 and S0.3 serve the same purpose -- "resolve all strategy and procurement questions before deploying anything." The stage-gate model separates them by timing; the outcome model groups them by purpose. No work is lost.
Reporting: When leadership asks "Is S1 done?", the answer comes from VI-OC-02 status. When they ask "Is S0 done?", the answer is: VI-OC-01 complete AND VI-OC-02 licensing deliverables complete.

SPLIT S2: Infrastructure Monitoring Modernized -> VI-OC-03 + VI-OC-04 + VI-OC-05

To VI-OC-03 (Platform Deployed): WP 2.1 (Tenant Setup), WP 2.2 (ActiveGate), WP 2.5 (PagerDuty Integration), WP 2.6 (Status Page)
To VI-OC-04 (Host Fleet Instrumented): WP 2.3 (OneAgent Deployment), WP 2.4 (OpenEdge/PASOE Monitoring)
To VI-OC-05 (Infra Visibility Validated): WP 2.7 (Initial Dashboards) + 30-Day Validation Gate
Why split: This is the most impactful split. In the stage-gate model, S3 (log migration) and S4 (synthetic monitoring) wait for all of S2 to finish. In the outcome model, VI-OC-06 and VI-OC-07 only wait for VI-OC-03 (platform deployed), not for VI-OC-04 or VI-OC-05. This unlocks 2-4 weeks of parallel execution.
Reporting: S2 is "done" when VI-OC-05 (30-day validation) passes. VI-OC-03 and VI-OC-04 completion can be reported as S2 sub-milestones. The 30-day validation gate remains the S2 exit criterion.

SPLIT S6: Baselines and Data Quality -> VI-OC-09 + VI-OC-12

To VI-OC-09 (Baselines Validated): WP 6.1 (Infra Baseline), WP 6.2 (App Baseline), WP 6.3 (Synthetic Baseline), WP 6.4 (SLO Definition), WP 6.7 (PagerDuty AIOps), WP 6.8 (Baseline Review), WP 6.9 (Data Quality)
To VI-OC-12 (Legacy Decommissioned): WP 6.5 (Elastic Decommission), WP 6.6 (CheckMK Decommission Eval)
Why split: Decommission gates on BOTH VI-OC-05 (infrastructure validated) AND VI-OC-06 (log migration validated). Baselines gate on VI-OC-05, VI-OC-07, and VI-OC-08. Different dependency chains, different purposes. Keeping them in one stage obscures the fact that decommission can proceed even if advanced baselines are delayed.

NEW VI-OC-13: A2W Monitoring Readiness

Consolidates from: S0 WP 0.5 (A2W Telemetry Strategy), S4 WP 4.7 (A2W Synthetic Readiness), S5 WP 5.7 (RUM Design), S8 WP 8.3 (RUM Validation)
Why new: A2W work is scattered across 4 stages because of timing. But it serves one purpose: "Be ready to monitor A2W when it launches." A Conditional outcome makes this trackable without creating phantom blockers. The constraint model can continue to reference A2W tasks in their respective stages.

NEW VI-OC-15: Legacy Fallback Activated

No stage equivalent. The constraint model mentions fallbacks in narrative text (e.g., "If the answer is no, the team pauses, addresses gaps") but has no dedicated stage for what happens when validation fails after max iterations.
Why new: Without this, the plan for "what if Dynatrace doesn't exceed CheckMK after 2 validation cycles" is implicit. VI-OC-15 makes it explicit: trigger condition, deliverables (root cause analysis, revised plan), and escalation path. Low-risk addition -- it doesn't change the constraint model, it fills a gap.

NEW VI-OC-16: PagerDuty AIOps Deployed & Validated

Partial reference in constraint model: S6 WP 6.7 (PagerDuty AIOps Confirmation) was a single verification task. VI-OC-16 captures the full lifecycle: phased deployment (alert grouping, auto-pause, expansion to all services), 30-day ML learning period, tuning, handover to DMSi, and team communication.
Why new: The PagerDuty AIOps rollout is already underway (started Week 1) and runs parallel with the Dynatrace deployment. It has its own phased plan (4 phases), validation cycles, ground rules, and handover process. A single WP cannot capture this scope. VI-OC-16 makes it a first-class trackable outcome with explicit dependencies on VI-OC-03 (PagerDuty integration) and feeding into VI-OC-10 (alerting optimization).
